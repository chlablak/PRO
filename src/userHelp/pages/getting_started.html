Getting started

Declaring a new graph

The first thing you will need to do when using Graph is declare a new graph. An empty graph is simply declared by typing:

[graph name] = {};

You can also declare a graph with vertices and edges like so:

g = {0,1,2,0--1,0--2};

The numbers alone (0, 1 and 2) represent vertices, whereas the numbers linked with two hyphens (0--1 and 0--2) are edges. More on this in the edge and vertex pages.

Once your graph is declared, you can apply an algorithm to it by typing the name of a new graph, followed by an equal sign and the method you wish to apply e.g.:

h = dijkstra(g, 1);

In this example, the second argument (1) is the edge from which to start.
